## (a) Target keyword phrases / search angles (intent-validated)

Pulled from SERP titles and result clustering. Grouped by intent so the pillar can map each to a section.

### Core / head terms (informational, high competition)
1. `content repurposing strategy` — the spine term; nearly every ranking page targets it.
2. `content atomization` — the "one big idea → many atoms" framing; distinct, less saturated than "repurposing."
3. `content distribution strategy` — covers the publish-and-amplify half people conflate with repurposing.

### Modifier / long-tail (lower competition, clearer intent)
4. `content repurposing strategy for founders` — explicit audience match; founder-led content angle.
5. `content repurposing for creators` / `repurposing content for solopreneurs` — solo-operator framing.
6. `how to turn one piece of content into many` — the canonical "1 → N" query; spawns "1 into 7 / 10 / 20" variants.
7. `one piece of content multiple platforms` — distribution-flavored cousin of #6.
8. `how to repurpose a blog post` (and `repurpose long-form video / podcast into clips`) — format-specific entry points.
9. `content repurposing workflow` / `content repurposing checklist` / `content repurposing system` — process-seekers, high commercial-adjacent intent.
10. `content repurposing examples` — proof-seekers; pairs well with case studies.
11. `best content repurposing tools` (+ `AI content repurposing tools`, `free content repurposing tools`) — bottom-funnel, tool-comparison intent.

### Adjacent / emerging
12. `how often should you repost content on social media` — distribution-cadence question; strong PAA pull.
13. `content atomization framework` / `content atomization playbook` — methodology-seekers who want a named system.
14. `founder-led content strategy` — wider bucket that captures repurposing as a sub-tactic.
15. `generative engine optimization` / `how to get cited by ChatGPT` — adjacent intent worth linking to (see AI-citation section); repurposing into structured, quotable blocks is now a distribution channel into AI answers.

**Diagnose takeaway:** Pick ONE head term to own (`content repurposing strategy` or `content atomization`) and 2–3 long-tail modifiers (`for founders`, `workflow/system`, `one piece into many`) as section anchors. The tool/comparison and cadence terms are separate satellite pages, not the pillar body.

## (b) "People Also Ask"-style question list

Reconstructed from SERP result titles, snippet framing, and recurring sub-headings across ranking pages. Use these verbatim as H2/H3s — they double as the GEO/AI-citation surface (see section d).

- What is content repurposing? (and how is it different from content distribution?)
- What is content atomization, and how is it different from repurposing? *(Repurposing = changing the format of one piece. Atomization = breaking one large idea into component "atoms," then building a multi-channel campaign around them.)*
- How do you turn one piece of content into many? / How many assets can one piece realistically produce?
- What's a realistic content repurposing workflow for a solo founder?
- Which content should I repurpose first? (How do I find my highest-value/winning piece?)
- What can you extract from a single pillar asset? (stats, quotes, frameworks, steps, case studies, contrarian takes)
- How often should I repost the same content on social media? (Per platform: Twitter/X same-day OK; Facebook wait ~30 days; Instagram every 3–6 months / 2–4×/year.)
- What are the best content repurposing tools? (And which one for blog vs. podcast vs. long video?)
- How do I repurpose a long video / podcast into short clips?
- How do I repurpose a blog post into social posts / newsletter / video?
- Does reposting the same content hurt reach or get flagged as spam?
- How is repurposing copy-paste different from platform-native adaptation?
- How do I build a repeatable system so repurposing doesn't depend on me?
- Can I repurpose content so it gets cited by ChatGPT / AI Overviews?

## (c) Pillar content angles (3–5)

Each is a defensible pillar with a distinct promise. Pick one as the pillar, demote the rest to supporting cluster pages.

1. **"One Idea, Many Outputs: The Atomization System for Founders"** — Lead on *atomization* (not just repurposing) to differentiate. Structure: Audit → Atomize → Reformat. Promise: one pillar asset → 15–30 platform-native pieces. Strongest GEO angle because it's a named, step-numbered framework AI can lift cleanly.

2. **"Create Once, Distribute Everywhere: The Solo-Founder Repurposing Workflow"** — Operations-first. A documented, partially-automated checklist + cadence calendar. Promise: turn one weekly pillar (1,500–2,000-word post or 10–15-min video) into a full week of multi-channel content without new ideas. Targets `workflow/system/checklist` cluster.

3. **"What to Repurpose First: Diagnosing Your Highest-Leverage Content"** — The leverage/prioritization angle (find the winning asset by traffic, intent alignment, conversion/low-CPA) before you scale effort. Directly maps to a pillar's **Diagnose** step. Lower competition, high "aha."

4. **"Platform-Native, Not Copy-Paste: Adapting One Asset Across 7 Channels"** — Distribution craft. Per-channel adaptation rules + reposting cadence per platform. Captures the cadence/`how often to repost` and `multiple platforms` intents.

5. **"Repurposing for the AI Era: Turning Content Into Citable Atoms"** — Bridges repurposing + GEO. Reframe repurposing as also distributing into AI answer engines via structured, quotable, sourced blocks. Newest/most differentiated; ties section (d) into the body.

**Recommended pillar:** #1 (atomization system) as the hub, with #3 (diagnose/prioritize) as the lead-in section and #2/#4/#5 as cluster pages linking back.

## (d) AI-citation section (what gets cited/ranks for these queries)

### What kinds of sources tend to rank / get cited
Observed pattern across the live SERPs and confirmed by the GEO sources searched:

- **Named-framework, step-numbered "playbook" pages win.** Ranking titles lean on "Playbook," "Ultimate Guide," "Framework," "Step-by-Step," and explicit counts ("One Idea, Dozens of Deliverables," "1 into 20 Assets," "One Piece, Ten Formats"). AI engines lift self-contained, numbered blocks; so do featured snippets.
- **Self-contained, liftable structure.** Content with clear headings, bullets, and tables is reported ~28–40% more likely to be cited because a model can extract one block without ambiguity. Lead each section with a direct 40–60-word answer.
- **Data + citations + quotes in the body.** Pages that themselves cite credible sources and include concrete stats ("a 3,000-word report yields 15–30 pieces," per-platform repost intervals) earn a ~30–40% citation/visibility lift. Sourced claims read as lower-risk for a model to repeat.
- **Freshness signals.** Visible publish/update dates and "2026" in titles correlate with ranking for these queries — the space refreshes annually.
- **Mix of source types ranks:** vendor/SaaS blogs (repurpose.io, Descript, Aprimo, Storyteq, Frase), agency blogs (Bluetext, Hashmeta), and creator/operator newsletters & Medium/Substack posts (practitioner credibility). Encyclopedic comprehensiveness favors ChatGPT citations; recency + concrete examples favor Perplexity; already-ranking pages feed Google AI Overviews.

### How to engineer the pillar to be cited (Diagnose-step requirements)
- Name the framework and number the steps (Audit → Atomize → Reformat).
- Open every H2 with a one-paragraph direct answer (40–60 words) before elaborating.
- Add a stat with a source roughly every 150–200 words; include at least one comparison table (e.g., repost cadence by platform; format-to-channel matrix).
- Add FAQ section using the section (b) questions; consider FAQPage + Article schema.
- Stamp a visible "Last updated 2026" date.

2. **Bluetext — "The Content Atomization Playbook: One Idea, Dozens of Deliverables"** — the named-playbook + Audit/Atomize/Reformat structure that ranks and gets lifted. https://bluetext.com/blog/the-content-atomization-playbook-one-idea-dozens-of-deliverables/
3. **Frase — "Mastering AI Citations: The Ultimate GEO Playbook"** — authoritative on *how* to get cited (structure, 40–60-word answers, stats/quotes, schema). https://www.frase.io/blog/how-to-get-cited-by-ai-search-engines-the-complete-geo-playbook
